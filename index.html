How this works — 3 steps
1. Import Sheet — drop your drawings (SVG, photo, blueprint). Drop several; each becomes a page.
2. Reference & Trace — draw one loop around the shape in each view: Side (from the side), Top (from above), Front (head-on).
3. 3D Preview — your object appears. Drag to spin it.
Your drawing always wins. The sliders below are just a rough lump to start from before you trace.
Rough starting proportions:
The finished measurements. After you trace, these show what your drawing measured — move them to resize and your tracing is left alone.
2Side profile · how tall▶
From: the sliders — trace a side view to use your drawing instead.
3Width · how wide▶
From: the sliders — trace a top view to use your drawing instead.
4Shape style · smooth or exact▶
Smooth — a flowing, rounded body. Best for cars, boats, anything streamlined. Features here are dents and bumps.
Follow my drawing — carves out exactly what your outlines say, corners and all. Use it for angular things, and it's the only mode that can cut a window clean through.
Only used by Follow my drawing. High = hard, machined corners. Low = softened.
5Features · none yet▶
Detail and joins live on the drawing, not in here. Open Reference & Trace: pick a tool in the toolbar, draw the shape, then a small panel opens over the view with that feature's own settings — so you can see what you're changing while you change it. Drag its box to move it, a corner to resize, an orange point to bend it.

Join is how parts become a toy: click where a peg or a socket goes and give it a size. The same size on two parts is made to fit once printed — the socket is cut wider by the printer's clearance so it doesn't seize solid.
⚙Advanced · detail & thickness▶
Only touch these if you want to. Higher detail = smoother but slower.
How thick the frame is. Nothing pressed in is allowed to go deeper than this — a 1 mm scoop needs more than 1 mm of frame. Tick cut clean through on a feature when you actually want a hole.
Blends where faces meet, so there's no seam. The thinnest face is what limits how deep a feature can press.
6Cross-section · the shape end-on▶
A cross-section is pinned at each cut; the model morphs between them. Front & rear views become cuts automatically. Drag the dots to reshape the selected cut — this edits the 3D model live.
From: the sliders — trace a front view for the real end-on shape.
No section cuts yet — trace a front view, import a sheet, or add one.
7Save & export▶
Keeps it on this device and in the shared cloud, and it appears in the Workshop. Saving again with the same name updates it instead of making a copy.
Everything on this page works without a backend, including real cut-through openings (use Follow my drawing). Build exact is an optional extra: it rebuilds the same outlines with a CAD kernel (OpenCascade) with no grid at all — corners exact at any zoom — and hands back a STEP file you can keep editing in any CAD package.
☁Cloud saves▶
Saves the full model — trace, drawings and all — so it follows you across devices. It should be wired in at deploy so nobody ever types a key; see SETUP.md. ▸ show setup SQL
